## Early Blight (Alternaria solani)

**Affected crops:** Tomato, Potato, Eggplant

**Symptoms:**
- Dark brown to black spots with concentric rings (target-board pattern)
- Yellow halo surrounding each lesion
- Starts on older, lower leaves and progresses upward
- Severe infection causes defoliation and fruit lesions

**Causal agent:** Fungus *Alternaria solani*; favoured by warm (24–29°C), humid conditions with leaf wetness

**Organic treatment:**
- Neem oil spray: 2–3 ml/L water, apply every 7 days
- Copper-based Bordeaux mixture (1%): preventive spray before symptom onset
- Trichoderma-based biocontrol: 4 g/L as soil drench and foliar spray

**Chemical treatment:**
- Mancozeb 75% WP: 2 g/L water; apply every 10–14 days
- Chlorothalonil 75% WP: 2 g/L water
- Azoxystrobin 23% SC: 1 ml/L water (rotate with other fungicides)

**Prevention:**
- Remove and destroy infected leaves; do not compost
- Avoid overhead irrigation; use drip irrigation
- Maintain plant spacing (45–60 cm) for airflow
- Crop rotation with non-solanaceous crops for 2–3 years

## Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)

**Affected crops:** Tomato, Potato

**Symptoms:**
- Water-soaked, pale green to brown lesions on leaves; white mould on underside in humid conditions
- Rapid spread — can destroy a field in 7–10 days under favourable conditions
- Dark brown lesions on stems and petioles
- Infected fruit shows firm, greasy brown patches

**Causal agent:** Oomycete *Phytophthora infestans*; favoured by cool (10–20°C), wet weather

**Organic treatment:**
- Copper oxychloride 50% WP: 3 g/L water (moderately effective)
- Remove and destroy infected plants immediately to prevent spread

**Chemical treatment:**
- Metalaxyl + Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold): 2.5 g/L water
- Dimethomorph 50% WP: 1 g/L water
- Cymoxanil + Mancozeb: 2.5 g/L water

**Prevention:**
- Plant resistant varieties (e.g., Arka Rakshak for tomato)
- Avoid excess nitrogen fertilisation
- Scout fields regularly during cool, wet weather
- Ensure good drainage; avoid water stagnation

## Powdery Mildew

**Affected crops:** Cucumber, Squash, Grapes, Wheat, Peas

**Symptoms:**
- White powdery fungal growth on upper leaf surface
- Yellowing and curling of affected leaves
- Stunted growth; premature leaf drop in severe cases
- Unlike most fungal diseases, occurs in warm, dry conditions with high humidity

**Causal agent:** Various *Erysiphe* and *Sphaerotheca* species depending on host

**Organic treatment:**
- Potassium bicarbonate solution: 5 g/L water, spray weekly
- Neem oil: 5 ml/L water + 1 ml/L liquid soap as emulsifier
- Milk spray: 1 part milk to 9 parts water (proven effective in cucurbits)
- Wettable sulphur 80% WP: 2 g/L water (avoid in temperatures >35°C)

**Chemical treatment:**
- Tebuconazole 25.9% EC: 1 ml/L water
- Hexaconazole 5% SC: 2 ml/L water
- Propiconazole 25% EC: 1 ml/L water

**Prevention:**
- Grow resistant varieties where available
- Ensure adequate plant spacing
- Avoid high nitrogen fertilisation (promotes susceptible lush growth)

## Rice Blast (Magnaporthe oryzae)

**Affected crops:** Rice (all growth stages)

**Symptoms:**
- Leaf blast: diamond-shaped lesions with grey centre and brown border
- Neck blast (most damaging): dark brown lesion at panicle base causing "rotten neck"
- Node blast: black discolouration of nodes
- Can cause 70–100% yield loss in severe outbreaks

**Causal agent:** Fungus *Magnaporthe oryzae*; favoured by cool nights (17–22°C), high humidity, excess nitrogen

**Organic treatment:**
- Silicon supplementation (silica-rich soil amendments improve resistance)
- Trichoderma harzianum seed treatment: 4 g/kg seed

**Chemical treatment:**
- Tricyclazole 75% WP: 0.6 g/L water (most effective systemic fungicide for blast)
- Isoprothiolane 40% EC: 1.5 ml/L water
- Carbendazim 50% WP: 1 g/L water (for early leaf blast)

**Prevention:**
- Plant resistant varieties (IR-64, Samba Mahsuri)
- Balanced fertilisation — avoid excess nitrogen
- Drain fields periodically to reduce humidity
- Treat seeds with Tricyclazole before sowing

## Bacterial Leaf Blight of Rice (Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae)

**Affected crops:** Rice

**Symptoms:**
- Water-soaked to yellow stripe along leaf margins, progressing to the midrib
- Leaves turn straw-yellow and wilt (Kresek phase in seedlings)
- Bacterial ooze (yellowish droplets) on lesions in humid conditions

**Causal agent:** Bacterium *Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae*; spreads through irrigation water and rain splash

**Chemical treatment:**
- Copper oxychloride 50% WP: 3 g/L water
- Streptomycin sulphate + Tetracycline (Plantomycin): 1 g/L water
- Bleaching powder: 10 kg/ha dissolved in irrigation water

**Prevention:**
- Use certified disease-free seed
- Drain flood water from fields during active tillering
- Avoid excessive nitrogen; split application recommended
- Remove weed hosts from field bunds

## Tomato Leaf Curl Virus (ToLCV)

**Affected crops:** Tomato

**Symptoms:**
- Upward curling and cupping of leaves; leaves turn yellow-green
- Stunted plant growth; flower drop
- Fruit set is severely reduced
- Transmitted by whitefly (*Bemisia tabaci*)

**Causal agent:** Tomato Leaf Curl Virus (begomovirus); whitefly is the primary vector

**Management (no cure once infected):**
- Control whitefly vector immediately using:
  - Imidacloprid 17.8% SL: 0.5 ml/L water (systemic insecticide)
  - Yellow sticky traps: 12–15 per acre
  - Neem oil 5 ml/L as repellent spray
- Remove and destroy infected plants to prevent spread
- Use virus-resistant tomato varieties (e.g., Arka Rakshak, Pusa Hybrid-2)
- Reflective mulches reduce whitefly landing on plants

**Prevention:**
- Raise seedlings under insect-proof net houses
- Maintain field hygiene; remove crop debris after harvest

## Downy Mildew of Grapes (Plasmopara viticola)

**Affected crops:** Grapes

**Symptoms:**
- Yellow oily spots on upper leaf surface ("oil spots")
- White cottony mould on lower leaf surface (sporulation)
- Infected berries turn brown, shrivel, and fall ("leather berry")

**Causal agent:** Oomycete *Plasmopara viticola*; spreads rapidly during wet, warm weather (20–25°C)

**Organic treatment:**
- Bordeaux mixture 1%: apply before and after rainfall
- Copper hydroxide 77% WP: 2.5 g/L water

**Chemical treatment:**
- Fosetyl-Al 80% WP: 2.5 g/L water (systemic, moves into plant)
- Metalaxyl + Mancozeb: 2.5 g/L water
- Dimethomorph 50% WP: 1 g/L water

**Prevention:**
- Prune vines for good airflow; train on trellis systems
- Avoid dense canopies; remove basal leaves around fruit zone
- Apply preventive sprays before expected rainfall events

## Wheat Stripe Rust (Puccinia striiformis)

**Affected crops:** Wheat, Barley

**Symptoms:**
- Bright yellow-orange pustules arranged in stripes along leaf veins
- Pustules contain powdery yellow spores
- Infected leaves dry out; severe infection reduces grain filling significantly

**Causal agent:** Fungus *Puccinia striiformis*; favoured by cool temperatures (10–15°C) with moisture

**Chemical treatment:**
- Propiconazole 25% EC: 1 ml/L water
- Tebuconazole 25.9% EC: 1 ml/L water
- Mancozeb 75% WP: 2 g/L water (protective, apply early)

**Prevention:**
- Grow resistant varieties (HD-2967, PBW-550, WH-711)
- Timely sowing (avoid very early sowing in high-risk areas)
- Monitor fields from tillering stage onward

## General Fungicide Resistance Management

To prevent fungicide resistance buildup:
1. **Rotate** fungicide classes — do not use the same mode of action consecutively
2. **Alternate** between contact (Mancozeb, Chlorothalonil) and systemic (Propiconazole, Tebuconazole) fungicides
3. **Apply at correct rates** — under-dosing accelerates resistance selection
4. **Use mixtures** where possible — dual-mode products slow resistance development
5. **Observe pre-harvest intervals (PHI)** on all chemical labels

## Evaluation Framework for Agentic RAG Systems

*(For course project use — §IV of research paper)*

When evaluating the AgriSense system for the course report, measure:

**Hallucination Rate:** Percentage of responses containing claims not supported by retrieved context.
- Baseline LLM expected: ~30%
- RAG-only expected: ~19%
- Full agentic system expected: ~12%

**Factual Correctness (1–5 Likert scale):** Expert rating of whether the advice is agronomically accurate.

**Answer Relevance (%):** Does the response actually address what was asked?

**Citation Correctness:** Does the response correctly attribute information to the source document?

**Latency (seconds):**
- Baseline: ~2s
- RAG-only: ~4s
- Agentic (Plan + Draft + Reflect): ~6s
